# IRON EMPIRE
The largest of the [[Three Kingdoms]], the Iron Empire stands unrivaled in the art of warfare and the grim mastery of torture. Its great capital, [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Eseryn (ess·ee·rin)**|Eseryn]], serves as both the heart of industry and the seat of the [[Emperor Calian Lorringer|Emperor]]'s rule.

In the heart of the Empire, the province of [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Ferrum (fur·uhm)**|Ferrum]] stands as its ideological stronghold. Its people, bound by ancient lineages predating the rise of the Empire, are unwavering in their devotion to its doctrine. The [[Iron Empire#ORDER OF THE DIVINE FLAME|Divine Flame]] floods the streets with propaganda, stoking fear of the foreign menace from the [[Dreadlands#DREADLANDS|Dreadlands]] and calling for purification of the land. Meanwhile, the nobles revel in excess, driven not by duty but by schemes, indulgence, and their own unrelenting thirst for power.

The closer one gets to the capital, the tighter the grip of the Iron Empire and influence of the [[Order of the Divine Flame]] on its citizens. A mere century ago, much of this land was a patchwork of independent nations—until they were brought to heel under the Empire’s banner. But not all within its borders embrace the monarchy. The lower classes bear its weight through ruthless tax collectors and ever-watchful [[Order of the Divine Flame#Flamebearer|Flamebearers]] who stride through their lands, enforcing control.

The scars of conquest remain etched in the ruins of desecrated monuments, shattered statues, and stolen artifacts and works of art—grim reminders of what once was. Resistance has flared time and again, yet each insurgency has been crushed, ending in blood. Yet, even in the shadow of oppression, voices still rise, unyielding in their call for independence.

## CULTURE
The civilians of the conquered lands cling to the remnants of their rich histories—histories outlawed under the Iron Empire’s rule. Their defiance is woven into melodies, carried in the rhythms of dance, and savoured in the flavours of traditional cuisine. Yet within the heart of the Empire, cultural identity is bound solely to [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Ferrum (fur·uhm)**|Ferrum]], its lineage and legacy enforced as the only acceptable heritage.

War is woven into the very foundation of the Iron Empire, where knights and royalty display their status through their armour with unwavering pride. Fighters clad themselves in iron-plated suits—not merely for protection, but as emblems of rank and prestige. The higher one's standing in society, the more intricate and ostentatious their armour, its elaborate craftsmanship serving as a testament to both wealth and influence.

## EDUCATION
Education is a privilege of the nobility, while commoners rely on tradition and self-teaching. Parents pass down practical skills, history, and language, though formal schooling is scarce, with only the rare schoolhouse offering more.

Literacy is uncommon among the lower classes—most speak only the Common tongue, unable to read or write. Nobles, however, receive a broader education, mastering written Common, learning the [[Tu'Naut]] language, and gaining a rudimentary understanding of [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Kiertak (keer·tak)**|Kiertak]], the ancient Frostborn dialect.

## ENTERTAINMENT
Nobles indulge in lavish banquets and grand festivities, their gatherings often serving as displays of wealth and dominance. Tournaments are held for aristocrats to flaunt their combat prowess before the common folk—though more often, it is indentured commoners who spill blood for sport.

Fighting pits thrive across the land, where wagers are placed and lives are gambled away. Though outlawed, hidden establishments cater to the darkest appetites of the elite, offering torture and even execution for those willing to pay the right price.

## FOOD
Salt is a staple in common households, used to enhance simple meals and preserve food. Nobles, however, enjoy a far more refined palate, indulging in exotic spices imported from [[Tu'Naut]].

The cuisine is built on hearty staples—grains, starches, and an assortment of meats, including pork, cattle, and poultry. Yet, their true culinary pride lies in their aged cheeses, renowned for their craftsmanship and rich variety.

## RELATIONS
Allies are valued not for loyalty, but for the benefits they provide.

### Jörden
Renowned for their master-crafted weapons, unmatched in quality and precision. The Iron Empire frequently commissions the [[Jörden|Frostborn]] to forge their finest arms, though every deal must serve the tribes’ interests—nothing is given freely.

### Tu’Naut
A hub of indulgence and ingenuity, offering exquisite cuisine, skilled courtiers, and groundbreaking technological advancements. Their brightest minds are often enlisted to spearhead factory operations, driving forward the latest innovations in warfare.

## REGION
Vast wheat fields stretch across the land, interspersed with scattered forests that breathe life into the terrain. Towering mountain peaks rise along the shoreline, standing as sentinels over the waters, while great lakes from the north carve their way through the landscape, feeding the land’s bounty.

## EMPEROR
[[Emperor Calian Lorringer|Calian Lorringer]], the Iron Emperor and heir to the empire’s first ruler, [[Emperor Rhoq the First|Rhoq]], has grown old and frail, his strength fading with the years. Though he still wears the crown, true authority rests with [[Divine Archpriest Faraday|Divine Archpriest Faraday]], who commands in all but name.

## RELIGION
Led by [[Divine Archpriest Faraday]], a gluttonous and self-indulgent figure whose influence eclipses all opposition. Under his rule, the [[Order of the Divine Flame]] stands as the sole religion, branding all others as "devilry" and erasing them from existence.

[[Order of the Divine Flame|The Divine Flame]] will root out supposed evil whether it exists or not. Those suspected of heretical worship face merciless persecution—their guilt assumed, their suffering inevitable.

## SOCIETY
Cruelty runs deep in the people of [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Eseryn (ess·ee·rin)**|Eseryn]], their enjoyment of suffering only tempered by ensuring they are not its victims. It is no surprise that the [[Order of the Divine Flame]] was born here, forged in a culture that thrives on dominance and pain.

Among the commoners, ignorance is both a curse and a tool—low education correlates with the nation's high birthrates, ensuring a steady supply of labourers and fodder for the noble elite. Debt is a dangerous weight; those burdened by loans or reckless gambling may sign themselves into indentured servitude to a noble, pledging their lives in exchange for repayment. These contracts are deliberately prolonged, trapping individuals in a cycle of exploitation. In some cases, their very existence can be gambled away—offered a single “chance” at freedom through bloodsport, whether in a fighting pit or a deadly tournament.

## TECHNOLOGY
The Iron Empire has forged countless weapons and devices, many crafted not merely for combat, but with calculated precision to impose suffering. Among them, the [[Order of the Divine Flame]] wields instruments of interrogation—brutal creations designed for both punishment and persuasion.

Across the empire, vast factories rise, churning out metal moulds for the mass production of tools, armour, and weaponry. Among these innovations, colossal steam engines drive relentless industry, clearing forests with ruthless efficiency to fuel the ever-expanding dominion.
