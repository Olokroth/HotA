# HISTORY
A century ago (13 BFE), the continent of [[Radea]] was a mosaic of independent nations. Seeking absolute power, the war-hungry nation of [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Ferrum (fur·uhm)**|Ferrum]] ignited a brutal campaign of conquest, forging the formidable [[Iron Empire]] from the blood of its rivals. Refugees fled north, taking shelter among the resilient [[Jörden|Northern Tribes]], their harsh homeland a natural fortress against invasion. In defiance, several nations banded together, founding [[Tu'Naut]] (11 BFE) to resist [[Iron Empire|Imperial expansion]]. Others, desperate to escape the carnage, ventured into the western wastelands. There, they encountered a cosmic being and established **Shatterfall** at the site of an ancient meteor, made up of [[Null Stone|black stone]] (6 BFE).

For twelve long years, war ravaged the land. But as the conflict endured, something unnatural began to seep from the wastelands. [[Corruption]] took root—twisting people into monstrous forms, unleashing horrors never before seen, and awakening arcane forces. The [[Iron Empire]] soon faced an affliction, an insidious plague spreading through its cities. In response, the [[Order of the Divine Flame]] was founded to combat the growing sickness. What followed was the [[Great Purification]] (1 BFE)—a fanatical purge that consumed the empire’s focus.

With new threats rising and old grudges fading before an unknown enemy, war came to an abrupt halt. The three surviving nations—[[Tu'Naut]], the [[Jörden|Northern Tribes]], and the [[Iron Empire]]—brokered a fragile peace, forging the [[Three Kingdoms]] (1 FE). A treaty defined their borders, marking a vast and uninhabitable "no man's land" between their territories. Trade routes were established, and a unified [[Calendar|calendar]] set the course for a new era. Yet looming above all was the dire need for defense. To shield their lands from the corruption spilling from the [[Dreadlands]], construction of a colossal wall began—a barrier that would take 36 years to complete (36 FE).

Now, in the present day (87 FE), the [[Great Wall|great walls]] still hold, standing as humanity’s last safeguard against the darkness beyond. But the [[Corruption|corruption]] festers, growing stronger with each passing year. The [[Order of the Divine Flame]] continues its solemn crusade, sending pilgrims led by [[Flamebearer|Flamebearers]] into the [[Dreadlands#DREADLANDS|Dreadlands]], battling a supernatural force that refuses to be contained.

# DREADLANDS
People across the land revere [[Entities]]—creatures from the void realm that have found their way into the continent of [[Radea]].

In the Dreadlands, horror is woven into daily life. Its inhabitants are accustomed to the unnatural strange figures lurking in the dark, eerie sounds echoing through the wilderness. Unlike the sheltered souls of the [[Three Kingdoms]], they do not recoil from the unknown but embrace it, their mannerisms subtly shaped by years of exposure. Their unsettling quirks—an unnatural calm in the presence of blood, a lingering stare, a slow, measured speech, or smiles stretched just a little too wide—make them unnerving to outsiders.

In the [[Three Kingdoms]], fear feeds superstition. Tales of possession, monstrous beasts, and corruption run rampant. The common folk whisper that contact with a Dreadlander invites sickness—or worse, transformation into something inhuman. This paranoia fuels the [[Order of the Divine Flame]], giving their crusade purpose, their cleansing justification.

# JÖRDEN
Jörden, meaning "Frozen Land," is home to the Frostborn, a name born from a misinterpretation of their [[Kiertak]] greeting, [[Phonetic Dictionary#forsse (fors) buren (bur·en)|Forsse buren]]—"to come from nature." These tribes are renowned survivalists, masterful hunters, and expert weapon forgers, clad in furs and leathers for mobility in the unforgiving cold.

The ancestors of the Northern Tribes settled in the harsh North, drawn by its geothermal properties and a deep reverence for nature. As their numbers grew, families branched off to form new tribes, spreading across the land in search of resources.

When the [[Iron Empire]]’s conquest reached their borders, the Northern Tribes endured, shielded by brutal weather and an intimate knowledge of their terrain. They took in refugees fleeing certain death and waged relentless guerrilla warfare against the invaders. Beneath the snow lie countless fallen.

With the war’s end and [[The Sovereign Concord]]'s signing, the Northern Tribes were formally recognized as Jörden, one of the [[Three Kingdoms]], charged with defending the North. Many refugees remained, integrating into the Frostborn tribes, while others returned home—or to what little remained of it.

## CULTURE
Only the strong survive the North. Every year at the winter equinox, all the tribes gather in the centre of the “[[Stone Crown]]" for a trial of all the children who've reached their 16th winter. They must survive a several-day journey through the harsh environment and reach the peak of the great volcano, [[Phonetic Dictionary#Eldurjall (el·dr·yall)|Eldurjall]]. A small cave exists near the peak where a violet flower blooms. The participants must return with one to prove their strength and resilience. Parents can decide to spare their children from an uncertain fate, but only by sending them away before the trial, exiling them from their tribe, usually to [[Tu'Naut]]. Only those who succeed in the trial can stay with the tribe.

## EDUCATION
Village elders are tasked with teaching the youth histories written onto copper tablets shared among all the tribes. The tablet histories rotate among the tribes each season.

Each family is responsible for passing down knowledge of combat, survival and craftwork to their children. Everyone must be skilled in each trade.

Frostborn learn the Common language, but they aren’t as practiced in it. They speak and write in [[Kiertak]], the old language of their people, and have a secret sign language, “[[Ekyst]]”.

## ENTERTAINMENT
Tests of strength and fierce brawls fuel high-stakes betting, while canoeing and ice fishing serve as both sport and survival. Intricately carved strategy games challenge the mind, alongside intellectual puzzles and handcrafted toys that entertain and sharpen young minds.

## FOOD
Vegetables thrive in geothermally heated caves, nourished by enriched soil, ensuring a steady supply despite the harsh climate. Livestock are sheltered in sturdy barns, safeguarded from the unforgiving weather. The waters surrounding the land teem with fish, providing an abundant and reliable food source.

## RELATIONS
[[Jörden|Jörden]] is rich in rare minerals and the master-crafted weapons of the Frostborn are unrivaled, coveted by both the [[Iron Empire]] and [[Tu'Naut]].

### Iron Empire
Distrust runs deep. The [[Iron Empire]], relentless in its industry, shows no regard for nature or the creatures that inhabit it. Their sprawling factories belch thick black smoke into the skies, and their insatiable hunger for resources strips forests bare. The Frostborn view them with disdain, trading only when necessity forces their hand.

### Tu’Naut
The Frostborn hold little respect for the sun-warmed scholars of [[Tu'Naut]], seeing their lives as sheltered and comfortable. Yet, the ingenuity of [[Tu'Naut]] proves its worth—technology and rare spices flow in exchange for the Frostborn’s rare minerals, forging a pragmatic, if begrudging, alliance.

## REGION
A land of extremes—vast frigid wastelands stretch endlessly, punctuated by towering active volcanoes and steaming geothermal hot springs. Beneath the surface, sprawling cave networks wind through the earth, while ancient forests stand as silent witnesses to the land’s untamed past.

**Provinces:** N/A
**Capital:** [[Stone Crown]]

## RELIGION
The only nation where true freedom reigns—where all are seen as equals, bound not by status but by mutual respect. The land is sacred, revered as the source of their strength, and treated with care. They take only what is needed, honouring both beast and tree. Each act of sustenance is marked with a prayer, unique to the moment—whether for a hunt, a harvest, or a meal, a quiet gratitude whispered to the land that sustains them.

## SOCIETY
There are nine tribes, and each elects a household to speak on behalf of the tribe. At any time, the tribe can elect a new household, so representatives are incentivized to hold the best interests of the tribe to heart. No one tribe holds any more power than the rest, and the closest thing to a capital is “[[Stone Crown|Buren Klauk]]” (nature's grasp), also known as Stone Crown, located in the middle of the tribes. It's unoccupied, except for gatherings such as festivals, tournaments and meetings.

Each tribe has their traditions or focuses, but generally all are quite similar as they share histories, have communal festivities and marry between tribes.

**Tribes:** Each tribe is named after an aspect of nature—[[Phonetic Dictionary#**Eldi (el·dee)**|Eldi]] (Fire), [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Gelta (gel·ta)**|Gelta]] (Moss), [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Himinn (hymn)**|Himinn]] (Sky), [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Hraun (whor·uhn)**|Hraun]] (Lava), [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Jörð (yohrd)**|Jörð]] (Earth), [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Málmgrýti (mahl·gree·tee)**|Málmgrýti]] (Ore), [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Nótt (no·t)**|Nótt]] (Night), [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Snjór (suh·yun)**|Snjór]] (Snow), [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Streymi (stray·mee)**|Streymi]] (Stream).

**Exiled Tribe,** [[Phonetic Dictionary#Goreter (gore·uh·ter)|Goreter]] **(Gore-Eater):** During a harsh winter, they resorted to cannibalism and are said to be possessed by an insatiable appetite. They live in the mountains at the border and hunt unsuspecting human prey, eluding the Frostborn tribes.

## TECHNOLOGY
Geothermal heating sustains their homes, nurtures their gardens, and shelters their livestock, harnessing the volcanic heat to endure the unforgiving cold.

# IRON EMPIRE
The largest of the [[Three Kingdoms]], the Iron Empire stands unrivalled in the art of warfare and the grim mastery of torture. Its great capital, [[Eseryn]], serves as both the heart of industry and the seat of the [[Emperor Calian Lorringer|Emperor]]'s rule.

In the heart of the Empire, the province of [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Ferrum (fur·uhm)**|Ferrum]] stands as its ideological stronghold. Its people, bound by ancient lineages predating the rise of the Empire, are unwavering in their devotion to its doctrine. The [[Order of the Divine Flame|Divine Flame]] floods the streets with propaganda, stoking fear of the foreign menace from the [[Dreadlands#DREADLANDS|Dreadlands]] and calling for purification of the land. Meanwhile, the nobles revel in excess, driven not by duty but by schemes, indulgence, and their own unrelenting thirst for power.

The closer one gets to the capital, the tighter the grip of the Iron Empire and influence of the [[Order of the Divine Flame]] on its citizens. A mere century ago, much of this land was a patchwork of independent nations—until they were brought to heel under the Empire’s banner. But not all within its borders embrace the monarchy. The lower classes bear its weight through ruthless tax collectors and ever-watchful [[Flamebearer|Flamebearers]] who stride through their lands, enforcing control.

The scars of conquest remain etched in the ruins of desecrated monuments, shattered statues, and stolen artifacts and works of art—grim reminders of what once was. Resistance has flared time and again, yet each insurgency has been crushed, ending in blood. Yet, even in the shadow of oppression, voices still rise, unyielding in their call for independence.

## CULTURE
The civilians of the conquered lands cling to the remnants of their rich histories—histories outlawed under the Iron Empire’s rule. Their defiance is woven into melodies, carried in the rhythms of dance, and savoured in the flavours of traditional cuisine. Yet within the heart of the Empire, cultural identity is bound solely to [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Ferrum (fur·uhm)**|Ferrum]], its lineage and legacy enforced as the only acceptable heritage.

War is woven into the very foundation of the Iron Empire, where knights and royalty display their status through their armour with unwavering pride. Fighters clad themselves in iron-plated suits—not merely for protection, but as emblems of rank and prestige. The higher one's standing in society, the more intricate and ostentatious their armour, its elaborate craftsmanship serving as a testament to both wealth and influence.

## EDUCATION
Education is a privilege of the nobility, while commoners rely on tradition and self-teaching. Parents pass down practical skills, history, and language, though formal schooling is scarce, with only the rare schoolhouse offering more.

Literacy is uncommon among the lower classes—most speak only the Common tongue, unable to read or write. Nobles, however, receive a broader education, mastering written Common, learning the [[Tu'Naut]] language, and gaining a rudimentary understanding of [[Kiertak]], the ancient Frostborn dialect.

## ENTERTAINMENT
Nobles indulge in lavish banquets and grand festivities, their gatherings often serving as displays of wealth and dominance. Tournaments are held for aristocrats to flaunt their combat prowess before the common folk—though more often, it is indentured commoners who spill blood for sport.

Fighting pits thrive across the land, where wagers are placed and lives are gambled away. Though outlawed, hidden establishments cater to the darkest appetites of the elite, offering torture and even execution for those willing to pay the right price.

## FOOD
Salt is a staple in common households, used to enhance simple meals and preserve food. Nobles, however, enjoy a far more refined palate, indulging in exotic spices imported from [[Tu'Naut]].

The cuisine is built on hearty staples—grains, starches, and an assortment of meats, including pork, cattle, and poultry. Yet, their true culinary pride lies in their aged cheeses, renowned for their craftsmanship and rich variety.

## RELATIONS
Allies are valued not for loyalty, but for the benefits they provide.

### Jörden
Renowned for their master-crafted weapons, unmatched in quality and precision. The Iron Empire frequently commissions the [[Jörden|Frostborn]] to forge their finest arms, though every deal must serve the tribes’ interests—nothing is given freely.

### Tu’Naut
A hub of indulgence and ingenuity, offering exquisite cuisine, skilled courtiers, and groundbreaking technological advancements. Their brightest minds are often enlisted to spearhead factory operations, driving forward the latest innovations in warfare.

## REGION
Vast wheat fields stretch across the land, interspersed with scattered forests that breathe life into the terrain. Towering mountain peaks rise along the shoreline, standing as sentinels over the waters, while great lakes from the north carve their way through the landscape, feeding the land’s bounty.

**States:** [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Ferrum (fur·uhm)**|Ferrum]], [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Erre (air)**|Erre]], [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Gao (gow)**|Gao]], [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Uul (ool)**|Uul]], [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Serren (ser·in)**|Serren]], [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Tyran (tier·in)**|Tyran]], [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Vorz (vors)**|Vorz]], [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Zerj (surj)**|Zerj]], [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Faseer (fa·seer)**|Faseer]], [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Pasdoor (paas·door)**|Pasdoor]], [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Karn (kar·nuh)**|Karn]]
**Capital:** [[Eseryn]], [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Ferrum (fur·uhm)**|Ferrum]]

## EMPEROR
[[Emperor Calian Lorringer|Calian Lorringer]], the Iron Emperor and heir to the empire’s first ruler, [[Emperor Rhoq the First|Rhoq]], has grown old and frail, his strength fading with the years. Though he still wears the crown, true authority rests with [[Divine Archpriest Faraday]], who commands in all but name.

## RELIGION
Led by [[Divine Archpriest Faraday]], a gluttonous and self-indulgent figure whose influence eclipses all opposition. Under his rule, the [[Order of the Divine Flame]] stands as the sole religion, branding all others as "devilry" and erasing them from existence.

[[Order of the Divine Flame|The Divine Flame]] will root out supposed evil whether it exists or not. Those suspected of heretical worship face merciless persecution—their guilt assumed, their suffering inevitable.

## SOCIETY
Cruelty runs deep in the people of [[Eseryn]], their enjoyment of suffering only tempered by ensuring they are not its victims. It is no surprise that the [[Order of the Divine Flame]] was born here, forged in a culture that thrives on dominance and pain.

Among the commoners, ignorance is both a curse and a tool—low education correlates with the nation's high birthrates, ensuring a steady supply of labourers and fodder for the noble elite. Debt is a dangerous weight; those burdened by loans or reckless gambling may sign themselves into indentured servitude to a noble, pledging their lives in exchange for repayment. These contracts are deliberately prolonged, trapping individuals in a cycle of exploitation. In some cases, their very existence can be gambled away—offered a single “chance” at freedom through bloodsport, whether in a fighting pit or a deadly tournament.

## TECHNOLOGY
The Iron Empire has forged countless weapons and devices, many crafted not merely for combat, but with calculated precision to impose suffering. Among them, the [[Order of the Divine Flame]] wields instruments of interrogation—brutal creations designed for both punishment and persuasion.

Across the empire, vast factories rise, churning out metal moulds for the mass production of tools, armour, and weaponry. Among these innovations, colossal steam engines drive relentless industry, clearing forests with ruthless efficiency to fuel the ever-expanding dominion.

# TU'NAUT
A nation of innovators and seafarers, the Southerners reject the constraints of religion, believing true enlightenment lies in the boundless exploration of the mind. Governed by a parliament of intellectuals, each member nation holds a seat, ensuring a balance of wisdom and progress. Justice is not dictated by rulers but upheld through fair trials, where criminals stand before their peers and receive proper legal representation.

Violence is scorned, for the people of Tu’Naut hold reason and intellect above brute force—proof that strength need not be measured in bloodshed.

## CULTURE
Tu’Naut is a haven for free thinkers—bold individuals with sharp minds and strong convictions. A nation steeped in knowledge and philosophy, it thrives on intellectual discourse and the pursuit of progress.

Its diverse cultures are bound not by uniformity, but by mutual respect, celebrating differences as the foundation of their unity.

Along its coasts, skilled shipwrights and seasoned sailors drive the nation’s maritime industry, mastering transport and fishing with precision and innovation.

## EDUCATION
The government-established schools are free and open to all, covering education from early learning to advanced adult studies. Supervised public records are displayed, ensuring transparency. Every individual raised in Tu’Naut is taught to write fluently in both their native tongue and the Common language.

The exclusive school [[House of Velinsta|Velinsta]], in the capital of [[Kata]] is well renowned for its education in the art of carnal pleasures. Those who graduate from the school are known as Vels and Vens—titles denoting females and males, respectively. Renowned for their beauty, grace, and intellect, they are widely admired and frequently sought after—even as desirable prospects for marriage. All business is overseen directly by the [[House of Velinsta]], guided by its matrons and patrons. The house operates as a political force, weaving intricate schemes to bolster the nation’s ambitions.

## ENTERTAINMENT
A culture rich in strategy and artistry. Grand tournaments showcase mastery of complex tactical games, while musical performances, intricate board games, and fine art provide both recreation and refinement. Reading, too, is highly valued, serving as a gateway to knowledge and imagination.

## FOOD
Their cuisine thrives on bold flavours and fresh ingredients—exotic spices and fragrant herbs elevate every dish, while bountiful seafood, rice, vibrant fruits, and lush vegetables sustain their people.

## RELATIONS
They uphold the philosophy of mutual growth, forging alliances built on shared knowledge and prosperity.

### Frostborn
An invaluable trade partner, exchanging advanced technologies and intellectual pursuits for the rare minerals for technologies.

### Iron Empire
Though vastly different in values, they recognize the Iron Empire’s industrial prowess. On occasion, they commission their factories to manufacture innovative designs, securing access to new engineering marvels.

## REGION
A land of warmth and contrast—sun-drenched deserts and glimmering oases offer sanctuary, while sprawling isles dot the coastline. With effortless access to the sea, their dominion thrives on exploration, trade, and maritime strength.

**Countries:** [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Su’Nunn (sue·noon)**|Su’Nunn]], [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Qwen (/k/·when)**|Qwen]], [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Ra’Quin (raw·quin)**|Ra’Quin]], [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Yew’Lin (yoo·lin)**|Yew’Lin]], [[Phonetic Dictionary#**K’Llo (kuh·low)**|K’Llo]], [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Aa’Sin (ah·sin)**|Aa’Sin]]
**Capital:** [[Kata]], [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Su’Nunn (sue·noon)**|Su’Nunn]]

## RELIGION
Faith is scarce, a quiet presence rather than a guiding force. Those who practice tend to have ties to foreign lands, hail from border towns, or have glimpsed the horrors beyond the wall and survived—changed by what they saw.

## SOCIETY
The birth rate is moderately lower than in the [[Iron Empire]], as priorities differ. While many pursue education with dedication, others see it as secondary to trade, craftsmanship, or survival.

## TECHNOLOGY
Innovation flourishes, driven by both necessity and ambition. Warfare evolves with the volatile force of explosive black powder and newly designed single-shot projectile weapons. Precision lenses sharpen sight for navigation and study, while advanced shipbuilding expands the frontiers of maritime exploration—yet breakthroughs continue to emerge.

The latest technological breakthrough is underway in [[Kata]], where scientists have devised a method to harness the kinetic energy of running water, storing it within concealed metal containers for later use. Similarly, sunlight can now be captured and preserved, powering bulbs that serve as steady torches during the dark hours.

# HERITAGE SUMMARY
Each heritage offers a distinct world view, evocative roles, and a rich tapestry of history that shapes every decision you make. Here’s why you might want to run one of these heritages:

## FROSTBORN
If the call of nature, ancient tradition, and survival in the face of relentless adversity stirs your soul, the Frostborn heritage offers a path like no other. Born of the harsh northern landscapes, the Frostborn hold deep reverence for their ancestors, nurturing an unbreakable bond with the land. Their story is one of endurance, honour, and the perpetual struggle against both the elements and the encroachments of modernity.

### Why choose Frostborn?
- **Primal Connection:** Become a hunter, tracker, or spiritual leader with deep-rooted ties to the natural world.
- **Tradition and Honour:** Live out a narrative where ancient customs and the honour of your kin serve as guiding pillars in a challenging environment.
- **Survival Against Tough Odds:** Whether leading your people or mastering the wilderness as a survivalist, experience the raw beauty and brutality of life in the North.

This heritage is perfect for players who crave a journey of rugged exploration, spiritual introspection, and enduring resilience.

## IRON EMPIRE
Imagine living in a realm where the very air is charged with rebellion, oppression, and a legacy of conquest. In the Iron Empire, every corner of the land is a reminder of a past when independent nations fell under the weight of an all-powerful regime. Whether you revel in the role of a dissenting rebel, a steadfast defender of an oppressive monarchy, or even an elite who challenges the status quo, your character will navigate a world where high stakes, political intrigue, and personal sacrifice are the norm.

### Why choose the Iron Empire?
- **Dynamic Conflict:** Engage in narratives fueled by the clash between oppressive authorities and the masses yearning for freedom.
- **Deep Class Tensions:** Decide whether your character stands with the downtrodden labourers or thrives as part of the noble elite.
- **Rich Historical Echoes:** Experience the weight of ancient conquests and the remnants of lost cultures that continue to haunt the present.

Whether you opt for the life of a knight, soldier, cleric, or even a humble miner or pilgrim, the Iron Empire promises a world where every action is underscored by historical scars and ambitious momentum.

## TU'NAUT
Step into a society defined by the brilliance of its diverse cultures and the power of intellectual audacity. The Tu’Naut heritage is a celebration of unity in diversity—where coastal cities are hubs of trade, invention, and secretive power plays. Here, the strength of a character isn’t measured by physical might but by the sharpness of the mind, the daring of their inventions, and the subtle influence exerted from behind the scenes.

### Why choose Tu’Naut?
- **Intellectual Prowess:** Play as an engineer, philosopher, scientist, or physician, where every discovery and innovation can tip the balance of power.
- **Cultural Fusion:** Embrace a heritage where differences are celebrated, offering diverse perspectives and intriguing backstories.
- **Undercover Intrigue:** Whether as a shipwright guiding maritime expeditions or a diplomat plotting power moves, delve into narratives full of covert maneuvers and quiet revolutions.  

This heritage is ideal if you see your character as a problem-solver, an inventor, or a mastermind whose intellect shapes the future in unexpected ways.

## IN SUMMARY
Each heritage is a portal into a world teeming with conflict, innovation, or timeless tradition:
- **Iron Empire:** For dramatic power struggles, political scheming, and narratives of rebellion or loyalty.
- **Tu’Naut:** For cerebral adventures, inventive breakthroughs, and the thrill of a culturally rich society.
- **Frostborn:** For a soulful connection to nature, a life of tradition and honour, and the grit required to survive harsh landscapes.  

Ultimately, your choice determines not just your role but the very lens through which you experience the game’s world. These heritages are more than just profiles—they are invitations to step into legends of rebellion, ingenuity, or survival. Which legacy will begin your destiny?

## SEASONS
There are three seasons: Bloom(ing), Reap(ing), and Wither(ing). Each season is 90 days and can be divided into three 30-day months/phases: Wax, Crest and Wane (e.g. Crest phase of the Bloom or Blooming season).

**BLOOM(ING): Season of Regrowth**
*Blooming, Wax (Budding)
Blooming, Crest (Unfolding)
Blooming, Wane (Maturity & Pollination)*

**REAP(ING): Season of Harvest**
*Reaping, Wax (Gathering)
Reaping, Crest (Threshing) 
Reaping, Wane (Storing)*

**WITHER(ING): Season of Decay**
*Withering, Wax (Wilting & Decay)
Withering, Crest (Withering & Breakdown)
Withering, Wane (Return to the Earth)*
