The Order was forged in fire and blood, born from fear and hatred. It began when [[Divine Archpriest Benjamin Kane|Benjamin Kane]], leader of a small sect, seized upon the fear of the people after an [[Void-Touched|altered]] individual was discovered in the great human kingdom of [[Eseryn]]. [[Divine Archpriest Benjamin Kane|Kane]] urged the [[Iron Empire#EMPEROR|Emperor]] to purify the afflicted soul through public execution, igniting a frenzy that would consume the city.

Thus began the [[Great Purification]]. Men, women, and children—any who bore [[Mutation|Mutations]], deformities, or simply fell out of favour—were hunted through the streets. Gathered in massive iron cages in the city’s centre, they were drenched in oil and burned alive, their agonized screams fuelling the righteous fervour of the masses.

The religion solidified its place in history, growing into a militant order with missionaries spreading its doctrine and fanatics rooting out [[Corruption|corruption]] wherever it lurked. At its head stood the [[Flamebearer|Flamebearers]], the blades of the church.

### Dreadlands
[[Flamebearer|Flamebearers]] see the [[Dreadlands]] as a blighted realm, a festering wound that must be purged. Holy pilgrimages are conducted to eradicate as much corruption as possible.

### Jörden
The [[Jörden|Frostborn]] refuse [[Flamebearer|Flamebearers]] entry, viewing their presence as an act of aggression. Unwilling to risk harm to their tribes, [[Jörden|Frostborn]] exiles who show signs of [[Corruption|corruption]] willingly banish themselves to the [[Dreadlands]], first accepting the treaty facial brand that marks them forever.

### Iron Empire
The homeland of the [[Order of the Divine Flame]], where [[Eseryn]] serves as its sacred stronghold. Here, its influence is absolute, shaping laws, governance, and the relentless pursuit of purity.

### Tu’Naut
[[Flamebearer|Flamebearers]] tread carefully within [[Tu'Naut]], knowing that without undeniable proof, their actions could incite the wrath of its people. Here, faith is met with scrutiny, and zealotry with resistance.
