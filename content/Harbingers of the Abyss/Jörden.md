# JÖRDEN

![[jorden.webp]]

Jörden, meaning "Frozen Land," is home to the Frostborn, a name born from a misinterpretation of their [[Kiertak]] greeting, [[Phonetic Dictionary#forsse (fors) buren (bur·en)|Forsse buren]]—"to come from nature." These tribes are renowned survivalists, masterful hunters, and expert weapon forgers, clad in furs and leathers for mobility in the unforgiving cold.

The ancestors of the Northern Tribes settled in the harsh North, drawn by its geothermal properties and a deep reverence for nature. As their numbers grew, families branched off to form new tribes, spreading across the land in search of resources.

When the [[Iron Empire]]’s conquest reached their borders, the Northern Tribes endured, shielded by brutal weather and an intimate knowledge of their terrain. They took in refugees fleeing certain death and waged relentless guerrilla warfare against the invaders. Beneath the snow lie countless fallen.

With the war’s end and [[The Sovereign Concord]]'s signing, the Northern Tribes were formally recognized as Jörden, one of the [[Three Kingdoms]], charged with defending the North. Many refugees remained, integrating into the Frostborn tribes, while others returned home—or to what little remained of it.

## CULTURE
Only the strong survive the North. Every year at the winter equinox, all the tribes gather in the centre of the “[[Stone Crown]]" for a trial of all the children who've reached their 16th winter. They must survive a several-day journey through the harsh environment and reach the peak of the great volcano, [[Phonetic Dictionary#Eldurjall (el·dr·yall)|Eldurjall]]. A small cave exists near the peak where a violet flower blooms. The participants must return with one to prove their strength and resilience. Parents can decide to spare their children from an uncertain fate, but only by sending them away before the trial, exiling them from their tribe, usually to [[Tu'Naut]]. Only those who succeed in the trial can stay with the tribe.

## EDUCATION
Village elders are tasked with teaching the youth histories written onto copper tablets shared among all the tribes. The tablet histories rotate among the tribes each season.

Each family is responsible for passing down knowledge of combat, survival and craftwork to their children. Everyone must be skilled in each trade.

Frostborn learn the Common language, but they aren’t as practiced in it. They speak and write in [[Kiertak]], the old language of their people, and have a secret sign language, “[[Ekyst]]”.

## ENTERTAINMENT
Tests of strength and fierce brawls fuel high-stakes betting, while canoeing and ice fishing serve as both sport and survival. Intricately carved strategy games challenge the mind, alongside intellectual puzzles and handcrafted toys that entertain and sharpen young minds.

## FOOD
Vegetables thrive in geothermally heated caves, nourished by enriched soil, ensuring a steady supply despite the harsh climate. Livestock are sheltered in sturdy barns, safeguarded from the unforgiving weather. The waters surrounding the land teem with fish, providing an abundant and reliable food source.

## RELATIONS
[[Jörden|Jörden]] is rich in rare minerals and the master-crafted weapons of the Frostborn are unrivaled, coveted by both the [[Iron Empire]] and [[Tu'Naut]].

### Iron Empire
Distrust runs deep. The [[Iron Empire]], relentless in its industry, shows no regard for nature or the creatures that inhabit it. Their sprawling factories belch thick black smoke into the skies, and their insatiable hunger for resources strips forests bare. The Frostborn view them with disdain, trading only when necessity forces their hand.

### Tu’Naut
The Frostborn hold little respect for the sun-warmed scholars of [[Tu'Naut]], seeing their lives as sheltered and comfortable. Yet, the ingenuity of [[Tu'Naut]] proves its worth—technology and rare spices flow in exchange for the Frostborn’s rare minerals, forging a pragmatic, if begrudging, alliance.

## REGION
A land of extremes—vast frigid wastelands stretch endlessly, punctuated by towering active volcanoes and steaming geothermal hot springs. Beneath the surface, sprawling cave networks wind through the earth, while ancient forests stand as silent witnesses to the land’s untamed past.

## RELIGION
The only nation where true freedom reigns—where all are seen as equals, bound not by status but by mutual respect. The land is sacred, revered as the source of their strength, and treated with care. They take only what is needed, honouring both beast and tree. Each act of sustenance is marked with a prayer, unique to the moment—whether for a hunt, a harvest, or a meal, a quiet gratitude whispered to the land that sustains them.

## SOCIETY
There are nine tribes, and each elects a household to speak on behalf of the tribe. At any time, the tribe can elect a new household, so representatives are incentivized to hold the best interests of the tribe to heart. No one tribe holds any more power than the rest, and the closest thing to a capital is “[[Stone Crown|Buren Klauk]]” (nature's grasp), also known as Stone Crown, located in the middle of the tribes. It's unoccupied, except for gatherings such as festivals, tournaments and meetings.

Each tribe has their traditions or focuses, but generally all are quite similar as they share histories, have communal festivities and marry between tribes.

**Tribes:** Each tribe is named after an aspect of nature—[[Phonetic Dictionary#**Eldi (el·dee)**|Eldi]] (Fire), [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Gelta (gel·ta)**|Gelta]] (Moss), [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Himinn (hymn)**|Himinn]] (Sky), [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Hraun (whor·uhn)**|Hraun]] (Lava), [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Jörð (yohrd)**|Jörð]] (Earth), [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Málmgrýti (mahl·gree·tee)**|Málmgrýti]] (Ore), [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Nótt (no·t)**|Nótt]] (Night), [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Snjór (suh·yun)**|Snjór]] (Snow), [[Phonetic Dictionary#**Streymi (stray·mee)**|Streymi]] (Stream).

**Exiled Tribe,** [[Phonetic Dictionary#Goreter (gore·uh·ter)|Goreter]] **(Gore-Eater):** During a harsh winter, they resorted to cannibalism and are said to be possessed by an insatiable appetite. They live in the mountains at the border and hunt unsuspecting human prey, eluding the Frostborn tribes.

## TECHNOLOGY
Geothermal heating sustains their homes, nurtures their gardens, and shelters their livestock, harnessing the volcanic heat to endure the unforgiving cold.
